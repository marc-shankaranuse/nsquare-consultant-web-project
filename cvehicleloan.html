Commercial Vehicle Loan

A commercial vehicle loan is a financing option specifically designed to help businesses or individuals purchase vehicles intended for commercial use, such as trucks, buses, vans, or other types of transport vehicles.

Key Features

  • Purpose: Used to buy vehicles for business purposes, such as transporting goods or passengers.
  • Eligibility: Businesses, fleet owners, individuals, or self-employed professionals.
  • Loan Amount: Typically covers 80-90% of the vehicle’s on-road price, depending on the lender and creditworthiness.
  • Interest Rates: Can vary based on lender, applicant's profile, loan tenure, and credit score. Generally, commercial vehicle loan interest rates are slightly higher than those for private vehicle loans.
  • Repayment Tenure: Usually ranges from 1 to 5 years, depending on the lender's policy and borrower preference.
  • Collateral: The purchased vehicle itself often serves as collateral for the loan.
  • Processing Fees: Lenders may charge a one-time processing fee for loan processing.
Types of Commercial Vehicle Loans
  • New Commercial Vehicle Loan: For purchasing new vehicles.
  • Used Commercial Vehicle Loan: For pre-owned vehicles, though the interest rate may be higher.
  • Top-Up Loan: For existing customers looking for additional financing on an existing commercial vehicle loan.
Eligibility Criteria
  • Age: Typically 21-65 years.
  • Income: A steady income source is essential to demonstrate repayment capability.
  • Business Stability: Lenders often look for a stable business history (at least 1-3 years).
  • Credit Score: A good credit score (usually above 650) improves chances for better rates.
Benefits
  • Capital Optimization: Helps conserve working capital by financing vehicle purchases.
  • Tax Benefits: Interest paid on a commercial vehicle loan may be tax-deductible as a business expense.
  • Increased Fleet: Enables small to medium businesses to expand their fleet without large upfront investments.
