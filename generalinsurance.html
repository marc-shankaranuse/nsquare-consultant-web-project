General Insurance

General insurance is a type of insurance that covers a broad range of non-life assets, such as vehicles, homes, health, travel, and businesses. Unlike life insurance, which is a long-term contract, general insurance provides financial protection for specific events and generally covers short-term periods, such as one year.

Types of General Insurance

  • Motor Insurance: Covers vehicles (two-wheelers, cars, commercial vehicles) for damages due to accidents, theft, natural disasters, or third-party liability.
  • Health Insurance: Provides coverage for medical expenses incurred due to illness or injury. Policies can cover hospitalization, critical illnesses, and, in some cases, day-to-day medical expenses.
  • Home Insurance: Protects the structure and contents of a home against risks like fire, theft, and natural disasters.
  • Travel Insurance: Covers trip cancellations, medical emergencies, and loss of belongings while traveling.
  • Commercial Insurance: Protects businesses against risks like property damage, liability, and employee-related risks.
