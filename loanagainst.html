Loan Against Property

A Loan Against Property (LAP) is a secured loan where borrowers use their residential or commercial property as collateral to get a loan from a bank or financial institution. This loan can be used for various personal or business purposes, offering higher loan amounts at lower interest rates compared to unsecured loans like personal loans.

Key Features of a Loan Against Property

  • Loan Amount: Typically 40-70% of the propertyâ€™s current market value, depending on the lender and borrower's profile.
  • Interest Rates: Lower than personal loans since the loan is secured by collateral. Interest rates can be fixed or floating.
  • Loan Tenure: Can range from 5 to 20 years, depending on the lenderâ€™s policy and the borrowerâ€™s repayment capacity.
  • EMIs: Monthly installments to pay back the loan, which include both principal and interest.
  • Type of Property: Both residential and commercial properties can be used as collateral, as long as the title is clear and the property is free of liens.

Common Uses for a Loan Against Property

  • Business Expansion
  • Medical or Educational Expenses
  • Debt Consolidation
  • Large Purchases or Events
Eligibility Criteria
  • Property Ownership: Clear title and ownership of the property.
  • Income and Employment Stability: Proof of income and repayment ability.
  • Credit Score: Generally, a good credit score (650+) improves eligibility.
  • Age: Usually between 21 and 65 years, but may vary by lender.
Tax Benefits
  • For Business Purposes*: Interest paid can be claimed as a business expense.
  • For Personal Use: Generally, there are no tax benefits unless the loan is used for buying or constructing a new property.
Advantages
  • Higher Loan Amount: Due to the secured nature, larger loans are possible.
  • Lower Interest Rates: Compared to unsecured loans like personal loans.
  • Flexible Repayment Terms: Allows for long repayment tenures, reducing monthly EMI burden.
  • Retain Property Ownership: You still own and can use the property, provided you meet repayment terms.
Considerations
  • Risk of Losing Property: In case of default, the lender can take ownership of the property.
  • Processing Time: More documentation and property valuation steps may make the process longer than a personal loan.
