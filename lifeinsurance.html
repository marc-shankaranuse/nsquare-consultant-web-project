Life Insurance

Life insurance is a financial product that provides a lump-sum payment to designated beneficiaries upon the policyholder's death, offering financial security to their family or dependents. Unlike general insurance, which covers assets or health for a specified period, life insurance is designed for long-term financial planning, often with investment or savings components.

Types of Life Insurance
  • Term Life Insurance:1. Provides coverage for a specified term (e.g., 10, 20, or 30 years). If the insured dies during this term, beneficiaries receive the death benefit. It is usually the most affordable option and does not have a savings component.
  • Whole Life Insurance: Offers lifelong coverage with a guaranteed death benefit. Premiums are usually higher than term policies, but the policy builds "cash value" that grows over time and can be borrowed against.
  • Endowment Plans:These policies combine life coverage with savings, providing a lump sum at the end of the policy term if the insured is alive, or to the beneficiaries if the insured passes away during the term.
  • Unit-Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs):Offer a mix of insurance and investment. A portion of the premium is invested in funds (equity, debt, or balanced), and the rest provides life coverage.
  • Money Back Policy*: Provides periodic payments, known as "survival benefits," throughout the policy term, with the remaining amount paid as a death benefit or at the end of the term.
  • Annuity/Pension Plans:Help policyholders save for retirement, providing regular payouts after retirement.
